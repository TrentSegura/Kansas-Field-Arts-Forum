The Kansas Field Arts Forum is a three-week intensive place-based pilot program collaboratively designed by the Kansas Creative Arts Industries Commission and M12 STUDIO, with generous support from Pratt Community College, and others. Designed as an experiential learning opportunity for emerging practitioners in the fields of New Rural Arts, Art & Ecology, Social and Environmental Art, and Creative Placemaking, participants in this program will visit numerous sites of social, ecological and cultural significance across the High Plains and Tablelands of rural Kansas.

Fifteen participants will be accepted to participate in the 2022 pilot program and will be notified by mid-May. The dates of the Forum are June 14-July 2 and require full-time residence in Pratt, Kansas during that period. Lodging is provided at no cost by Pratt Community College. Each person has private sleeping quarters and shares a non-gendered bathroom with their neighbor on campus. This program provides up to two meals per day at no cost through the college. Participants are required to send a non-refundable payment of $100 to secure their spot and help cover the cost of group travel activities. Participants are responsible for their own transportation to and from Pratt Community College, and should be prepared to cover the cost of some meals and most art supplies on their own.

The Kansas Field Arts Forum is intended to increase the appreciation and understanding of issues related to rural environments, provide a supportive atmosphere to engage and educate, and provide access to cutting edge curriculum and guest faculty members/practitioners. The Kansas Field Arts Forum culminates with an exhibition and community art loop at the Vernon Filley Art Museum.

The 2022 Kansas Field Arts Forum sites of engagement include the Gypsum Scenic Byway, Gant-Larson Ranch and Trails, Stone Bridges, Cheyenne Bottoms Wildlife Area, Hot and Cold Water Towers, Feedyard Overlooks, the MT Liggett Art Environment, Lemon Park, and the Pratt County Landfill. Completing the program application is not a commitment nor does it guarantee participation, it serves as an application, and expression of interest only. Apply at the top of the webpage.
